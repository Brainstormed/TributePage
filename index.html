Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The missile man of India

THE TIMELINE OF KALAM'S LIFE IS GIVEN BELOW:
  • October 15th 1931 - Born in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954 - Graduated in physics from Madras University of Technolgy
  • 1955 - Moved to Madras to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technolgy.
  • 1960 - Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (by Press Information Bureau, Government of India) as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS).
  • 1969 - Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980.
  • 1969 - Received the government's approval and expanded the programme to include more engineers.
  • 1963-1964 - Visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland; and Wallops Flight Facility.
  • 1970-1990 - Made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.
  • 1992-1999 - Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.
  • 1998 - Third President of India to have been honoured with a Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour.
  • 1998 - Along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent".
  • 25th July 2002-25th July 2007 - served as the 11th president of India, succeeding K. R. Narayanan.
  • 27th July 2015 - Death at the age of 83
